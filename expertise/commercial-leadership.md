# Commercial Leadership

> *Commercial leadership is not the management of sales. It is the capability to align strategy, people, customers and execution in order to create sustainable profitable growth.*

---

# Executive Perspective

Commercial leadership has become one of the most misunderstood disciplines in modern business.

In many organizations it is still considered synonymous with sales management. In reality, commercial leadership is considerably broader.

It is the discipline of designing an organization that consistently creates value for customers while delivering sustainable profitable growth.

That requires the alignment of commercial strategy, organizational capability, customer understanding, operational excellence and leadership.

Sales is only one component.

---

# Beyond Sales Leadership

Commercial leadership integrates multiple disciplines into a coherent business system.

It connects:

- Commercial Strategy
- Sales Leadership
- Marketing
- Business Development
- Pricing
- Customer Experience
- Key Account Management
- Supply Chain
- Operations
- Finance
- Human Resources
- Information Technology

When these functions operate independently, organizations become slower, more political and less customer focused.

Commercial leadership exists to align them.

---

# The Executive Responsibility

For me, commercial leadership begins with one simple question:

> **How can this organization create more value for customers than competitors while generating sustainable profitable growth?**

Every commercial decision should contribute to answering that question.

Leadership therefore extends far beyond revenue generation.

It includes:

- designing organizations
- developing people
- allocating resources
- setting priorities
- establishing governance
- building partnerships
- managing change
- creating accountability

---

# Commercial Leadership Across My Career

Throughout my career I have applied these principles across international organizations including Henkel, Davines Group and CWS Group.

The contexts differed significantly.

Some businesses operated through direct subsidiaries.

Others depended on distributor networks.

Some required rapid international expansion.

Others demanded organizational transformation in mature markets.

Despite these differences, the underlying leadership principles remained remarkably consistent.

Commercial leadership always required balancing strategic ambition with disciplined execution.

---

# What Commercial Leaders Actually Do

Effective commercial leaders continuously work across five dimensions.

## 1. Define Direction

Establish a clear commercial strategy aligned with long-term business objectives.

## 2. Build Capability

Develop organizations, processes and people capable of delivering that strategy.

## 3. Create Alignment

Ensure marketing, sales, finance, operations and supply chain pursue common objectives.

## 4. Execute Consistently

Translate strategy into measurable daily execution.

## 5. Continuously Improve

Adapt structures, capabilities and priorities as markets evolve.

# Typical Commercial Challenges

Across industries, organizations often struggle with similar problems.

Examples include:

- unclear commercial strategy
- fragmented customer ownership
- ineffective distributor management
- weak accountability
- inconsistent pricing
- siloed functions
- limited commercial transparency
- declining profitability despite revenue growth

These challenges rarely originate in one department.

They are usually symptoms of insufficient commercial leadership.

# My Leadership Approach

My approach is based on four principles.

## Simplicity

Complex organizations rarely execute well.

## Transparency

People make better decisions when objectives and responsibilities are clear.

## Empowerment

Strong organizations develop leaders at every level.

## Long-Term Thinking

Sustainable commercial success is created over years rather than quarters.
