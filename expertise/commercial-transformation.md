# Commercial Transformation

> *Commercial transformation is not about reorganizing departments. It is about redesigning how an organization creates value for customers while improving sustainable profitable growth.*

---

# Executive Perspective

Many organizations initiate commercial transformation because revenue growth has slowed, margins are under pressure or market conditions have changed.

Too often, however, the response focuses on restructuring organizational charts, introducing new systems or replacing leadership.

Those actions alone rarely solve the underlying problem.

Commercial transformation is fundamentally about improving the way an organization creates value for customers, translates strategy into execution and develops the capabilities required for future growth.

Successful transformation therefore begins with understanding the commercial system rather than individual departments.

---

# Why Commercial Transformations Fail

Most commercial transformation initiatives do not fail because of poor intentions.

They fail because organizations underestimate the complexity of changing interconnected systems.

Common causes include:

- unclear strategic priorities;
- insufficient executive alignment;
- fragmented ownership;
- inconsistent communication;
- excessive organisational complexity;
- lack of commercial accountability;
- transformation driven by structure rather than customer value;
- inadequate investment in leadership capability.

Technology alone does not transform organizations.

People do.

---

# Commercial Transformation as a Business System

Commercial transformation should integrate every major commercial function.

These include:

- commercial strategy;
- sales leadership;
- marketing;
- customer experience;
- pricing;
- key account management;
- distributor management;
- supply chain;
- operations;
- finance;
- information technology;
- human resources.

Transformation succeeds when these functions pursue common commercial objectives rather than local optimisation.

# Experience Across International Organizations

Throughout my career I have contributed to commercial transformation in multinational environments including Henkel, Davines Group and CWS Group.

Each organization presented different commercial challenges.

Some required international expansion.

Others required organisational redesign.

Several required the transformation of distributor-led business models into higher-performing commercial organisations.

Although the business context differed, one lesson remained constant:

Commercial transformation succeeds only when strategy, leadership, people and execution evolve together.

# My Commercial Transformation Model

I approach commercial transformation through six interconnected stages.

## 1. Understand

Analyse markets, customers, commercial performance and organisational capability.

Transformation should begin with evidence rather than assumptions.

---

## 2. Define

Establish a clear commercial ambition.

Success requires a shared understanding of what the organisation is trying to become.

---

## 3. Design

Develop the future commercial operating model.

This includes:

- organisation;
- governance;
- customer ownership;
- commercial processes;
- performance management;
- decision making.

---

## 4. Enable

Build the capabilities required for sustainable execution.

Examples include:

- leadership development;
- sales capability;
- commercial excellence;
- systems;
- reporting;
- incentives.

---

## 5. Execute

Translate strategy into disciplined implementation.

Execution requires:

- communication;
- accountability;
- measurement;
- adaptation.

---

## 6. Sustain

Transformation is complete only when new behaviours become normal operating practice.

Long-term capability matters more than short-term projects.

---

# Principles That Guide My Work

Every commercial transformation should improve three dimensions simultaneously.

## Better customer value.

## Better organisational capability.

## Better financial performance.

If one dimension improves while another deteriorates, the transformation remains incomplete.

---

# Typical Executive Questions

Commercial transformation often begins with questions such as:

- Are we organised for future growth?
- Are our commercial teams structured correctly?
- Are distributors still the right route to market?
- Should we establish direct subsidiaries?
- Where do responsibilities overlap?
- Which commercial capabilities are missing?
- How should commercial performance be measured?
- What operating model best supports our strategy?

These are leadership questions before they become organisational questions.

# Closing Reflection

Commercial transformation should never be viewed as a temporary project.

It is the continuous evolution of an organisation's commercial capabilities.

The organisations that outperform competitors over the long term are rarely those with the most ambitious transformation programmes.

They are those that continuously improve how strategy, people, customers and execution work together to create sustainable value.
